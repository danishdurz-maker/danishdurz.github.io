Task 2 · Comprehensive Descriptions

This section explains the purpose and key features of each technology in the built environment lifecycle and shows curated visuals (images and videos) to support understanding.

Purpose

BIM integrates all building disciplines into a single intelligent 3D model, enabling collaborative design, clash detection, and accurate documentation throughout the design phase.

Key Features

  • 3D coordinated modelling between architecture, structure and MEP
  • Clash detection (Revit / Navisworks)
  • 4D (time) and 5D (cost) simulation for project planning
  • Automatic generation of drawings and bill of quantities (BOQ)

Visuals

Purpose

Reality capture uses drones, photogrammetry and LiDAR to document site conditions and construction progress safely and efficiently.

Key Features

  • High-resolution orthophotos of the construction site
  • LiDAR point clouds for accurate as-built models
  • Progress tracking and earthwork volume calculations
  • Remote safety inspections without exposing workers to hazards

Visuals

Purpose

A digital twin of a tunnel or infrastructure asset connects the physical asset with a virtual model using IoT data to monitor safety, traffic and environmental conditions in real time.

Key Features

  • Live dashboards showing tunnel status and traffic flow
  • Integration of sensors for lighting, ventilation and safety systems
  • Simulation of emergency scenarios and response strategies
  • Historical data analysis to improve operations and maintenance plans

Visuals

Purpose

Predictive maintenance combines IoT sensors and CMMS to monitor equipment condition, predict failures earlier, and schedule maintenance at the right time.

Key Features

  • Condition-based monitoring using vibration, temperature and runtime
  • Automatic work order creation in CMMS
  • Analytics for mean time between failures (MTBF) and repair (MTTR)
  • Mobile inspections using QR/Barcode tags

Task 3 · Impact & Analysis

Advantages

  • BIM: Superior coordination, fewer clashes, improved documentation.
  • Drones: Safer inspections, faster progress tracking, accurate as-builts.
  • Digital Twin: Lower energy usage, optimised building performance.
  • Predictive Maintenance: Reduced downtime and smarter asset management.

Challenges

  • High setup cost (software, sensors, hardware).
  • Data interoperability issues (IFC, BACnet, etc.).
  • Training needs & industry readiness.
  • Cybersecurity concerns in IoT systems.

Impact Timeline (Example)

Design: BIM can reduce design clashes by ~30%.
Construction: Drone capture can speed up inspection by ~50%.
Operation: Digital twins can cut energy usage by 10–20%.
Maintenance: Predictive analytics can lower unplanned failures by ~25–40%.
